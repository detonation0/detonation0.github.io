The 2014 Flagship Killer

Posted on April 23, 2014
By Mathew Ruff

Last night OnePlus unveiled their new flagship device the OnePlus One. It’s been told it’s going to be the Nexus 5 killer, besting it in specs, price, and security. Get hyped.

The One boasts a Quad-Core Snapdragon 801 CPU @ 2.5GHz, a whopping 3GB of RAM, a 13 Megapixel Camera, and a 3100 mAh battery. These stats alone blow the Nexus 5 out of the water, which is saying something. The N5 has a Quad-Core Krait 400 CPU @ 2.3GHz, 2GB of RAM, an 8 Megapixel Camera, and a 2300 mAh battery. It just doesn’t compare to the One (Matrix reference ftw).
Another great feature is that the One runs CyanogenMod instead of stock Android. So along with all the great features of Android 4.4, users get the added security and functionality of CyanogenMod 11S. The screen is built using Corning Gorilla Glass 3, measuring in at 5.5" and 401ppi.
The OnePlus One will be offered in two sizes, a 16GB and a 64GB priced at $299 and $349 respectively. This undercuts the Nexus 5 by $50 for the 16GB and matches the 32GB price of $350! With the stats and price on this thing, it’s hard to stay with the N5. The One also lets you swap out the back plate for something a little prettier:
The One will be available starting today. OnePlus is also holding a "Smash" campaign that will allow people to sign up to smash their old phones for the opportunity to purchase a OnePlus One for $1. Would you smash your phone for the One?
