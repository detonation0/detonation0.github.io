Why Everyone Should Learn Code

Posted on Feb 20th, 2014
by Mathew Ruff

Lately I've been wanting to expand on my coding knowledge. I mean I know code, but I really want to work on key languages and areas. I've heard of a few places online to learn code before but a co-worker of mine recommended Codecademy. I figured hey why not, I haven't really refreshed my knowledge of code in a while. I signed up and started taking their free Javascript lessons. After a few days of going through refreshers on loops, arrays, variables, and the like I started to realize there were a bunch of areas that I didn't remember. Codecademy sets up the training in a helpful way. On the left side of the screen are the instructions for the current exercise, in the middle if where you input your code, and on the right side is the console that returns your output.




It's very simple to follow along with the exercises, and each level has multiple examples to give you plenty of practice. If you do get lost and don't know how to complete the problem all you have to do is click on the Q & A in the bottom left corner. That will take you to the forums where other people can post problems and solutions (without giving you the answer!). It even tracks your goals and gives you achievements for completing tracks in each coding language.



Okay great but why should I care?

You should care because we are increasingly getting closer to a world completely run by technology (we're basically there). Learning how this technology works is so important in an information society. Because we have access to technologies like this that teach us how to manipulate software, everybody should take advantage of it. Besides Codecademy is free and walks you through step-by-step in an interactive environment. Plus you can learn at your own pace. I hope you get the chance to at least check it out, if you've ever wanted to know how a website is built, how to query YouTube (or other websites) with just code, or how to make your own video game, I implore you to check it out. See you on the interwebs folks.
