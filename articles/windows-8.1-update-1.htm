Windows 8.1 - Update 1

Posted on April 10, 2014
By Mathew Ruff

The new Windows 8.1 Update has been out for a few days now bringing a ton of new features. I've gotta say, Microsoft is really starting to listen to it's users. We’ve have had the ability to to boot directly to the desktop since 8.1 was released, but now users can run metro apps from ‘within’ the desktop. Meaning when in a full screen application, the desktop taskbar is reachable on the bottom of the screen. Along with access to the taskbar we can also pin these metro apps to it, for quick access from the desktop.

This allows desktop users to stay on the desktop (you don’t say). Which for most Windows Veterans is a step in the right direction.

Every Customer I’ve ever introduced to Windows 8: “How do I close this program?”

When Windows 8 first came out most users were completely lost. That was because of a poor rollout strategy by Microsoft that left user without any documentation or help besides a 5 second video that played the first time they started their new machines up. But now Microsoft has added a title bar to metro apps that shows when you move your mouse to the top of the screen. On this bar they’ve added a small red ‘x’ in the top right-hand corner to close the app (instead of dragging it down). This makes it easier for users and reminds them of Windows 7 functionality.


“I use a mouse, why do I need gestures on my desktop?”

Possibly the best part of the upgrade is for mouse and keyboard users. The update adds two buttons to the start menu, one for power, and one for search. Instead of opening the charms menu by sliding your mouse into one of the right corners and then up or down, all a user has to do is click the Search button and the Search Charm will open up. Same goes for Power, one click and you have options for Shutdown, Sleep, and Restart.

The update is ~940MB for desktop users and ~490MB for Surface RT users. My Surface got the update and everything is the same as the desktop version aside from the power button being located on the Start Screen. I think Microsoft figured they’d leave that out because you’d be using the touch screen on that device. When you get the update, it’ll default booting to desktop instead of the Start Screen, you can change this by right clicking on the taskbar and changing the navigation settings (there’s a check-box for this option). It’ll also put the Windows Store on your taskbar which you can promptly remove, because who likes machines making decisions for us on what apps we want? Not me.

Because I love shortcuts I’ll share some tips when using Windows 8. Since it’s come out I’ve been using these to get around a lot quicker than using my mouse and the new gestures.

Here’s a table straight from Microsoft’s documentation, I bet you didn’t know about most of these:

To do this

 +D

 +E

 +F

 +H

 +I

 +K

 +L

 +M

Minimize all windows

 +O

 +P

 +Q

 +R

 +S

 +T

 +U

 +V

 +Shift+V

 +W

 +X

Open the Quick Link menu

 +Z

Show the commands available in the app


Whew, that was exhausting. Another great feature to mention is search. I’ve mentioned it at the top of the article but you actually don’t have to click the icon or swipe in from the right to access the search. Just like in Windows 7 and Vista before it, all you have to do is open the Start Menu and start typing what you’re looking for. The same goes for Windows 8. Just click  and start typing away.

Windows 8.1 Update 1 has brought a bunch of customization features to the operating system. I believe Microsoft has finally started redeeming itself in the eyes of it’s user, but we’ll have to see what happens in the future. What do you all think? Is Windows 8 really all that bad now?
