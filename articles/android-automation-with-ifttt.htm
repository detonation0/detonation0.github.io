Android Automation with

Posted on April 29, 2014
By Mathew Ruff

A while back I covered some automation using Zapier and IFTTT. Recently IFTTT (If This Then That) has added Android specific channels to it’s recipe creation page. This opens the door for tons of automated tasks on your Android device.

Before we dive into it, a little information on IFTTT. IFTTT lets you create “recipes" to automate certain functions of your technology. For example you can create a recipe that will send you a text message if it’s going to rain tomorrow so you can bring an umbrella. Or you can get an email when a new feed item is posted on your favorite website or YouTube channel. Examples:




Along with adding some new Android Channels, IFTTT has also come out with an Android App to manage your recipes from your device. With it you can browse recipes in different categories and customize your current recipes. I ended up snagging a few really cool recipes like: whenever I get a call add it to a log on Google Drive, or: when I get home set ringer volume to 60%. Another cool one: everyday at 11pm set ringer to silent. There’s almost no end to the possibilities.


IFTTT is completely free. So go check out the site and sign up. All you have to do to use a recipe is “activate” the channel and click use recipe. To activate a channel click activate and it will ask you to sign into that service. For example if I wanted to use my Dropbox, I just log in with my account and allow access from IFTTT. Simple. Easy. That’s how the automated world should be. What kind of recipes do you use? Feel free to share them in the comments. For now I’ll leave you with a few more to choose from.
