A New Push

Posted on August 4, 2014
By Mathew Ruff

Pushbullet - I’ve been meaning to write about the usefulness of this app for quite some time, so let’s get it started. Pushbullet is one of those apps that I use constantly. It allows you to push content between your devices quickly and easily. Say for example you are on your computer reading about a new app on Android Police and notice that they have the .apk file to download. Instead of finding the site again on your mobile and navigating to the download site, you could just click the Pushbullet icon in Chrome (or FF) and push it to your mobile.

Not only can you push links, you can also push notes, lists, pictures, and files. The design and layout of the website is user friendly as well. On the right side it shows a history all your pushes. On the left, the list of all your devices. So you can filter by device. It's really quick and easy to share pictures, mp3s, files, and wallpapers between your devices without having to use a PC or cloud service like Box or Dropbox. You can also push from your mobile to Desktop.

Perhaps the best feature is mirrored notifications. With the setting enabled, you'll see your phone or tablets notifications appear on your PC or Mac. You can even respond to the notifications from some apps. For example when you get a Facebook or twitter notification you can click the notification and it'll open the site in a new tab for you.

The Android and iPhone apps are laid out just like the website with easy access to individual pushes, or all pushes from all devices. The app integrates into the system share menu for sharing from within another app or service as well as the Chrome app, just like on the desktop version. Overall Pushbullet is probably one of the most useful apps you can find on stock Android. The Pushbullet team is constantly updating the app and they even have a Beta channel if you like to live on the bleeding edge. They are currently working on the ability to push to all of your devices and have just released a Windows desktop version of the app with right-click menu support for sharing files. So go download the app now, and start pushing.
